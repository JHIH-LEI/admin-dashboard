Lion

Lions are large cats found in Africa and parts of Asia. Known as the "King of the Jungle," they live in groups called prides. Lions are powerful hunters, relying on teamwork to catch prey, and have a distinctive mane around their head.

Elephant

Elephants are the largest land animals, known for their long trunks and large ears. Found in Africa and Asia, they are highly intelligent and social animals, living in tight-knit herds led by matriarchs. Elephants are herbivores and have excellent memories.

Penguin

Penguins are flightless birds that live in the Southern Hemisphere, especially Antarctica. They are well-adapted to swimming and diving, using their wings as flippers to propel themselves through water. Penguins are known for their playful nature and strong family bonds.

Giraffe

Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth, with long necks that help them reach leaves high in trees. Native to Africa, they have distinctive spotted patterns on their coats and are known for their gentle, social behavior. Giraffes communicate with low-frequency sounds.

Kangaroo

Kangaroos are marsupials native to Australia, famous for their powerful hind legs and large tails. They use their strong legs to travel long distances in search of food and water. Kangaroos live in groups called mobs and are herbivores, feeding on grass and plants.
